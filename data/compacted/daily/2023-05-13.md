## Beer Brewing Microbiology
- **Microorganisms**: 
  - **Saccharomyces cerevisiae**: Alcoholic fermentation, converts sugars to alcohol/CO2
  - **Lactobacillus**: Sour beers, produces lactic acid
  - **Pediococcus**: Sour beer production, lactic acid
  - **Brettanomyces**: Belgian lambics/saison, fruity/spicy notes
  - **Acetobacter**: Can create sour beer or vinegar if uncontrolled
- **Process Control**: Temperature maintenance (refrigeration), pH monitoring, oxygen control, yeast management (viability, nourishment), sanitation of equipment, avoiding splashing, using conical/cylindroconical fermenters for gravity transfer to secondary/tertiary vessels
- **Contamination Risks**: Infection (off-flavors, acetic acid/vinegar), under/over fermentation (sweet vs sour/alcohol taste), temperature fluctuations (esters/dormancy), oxidation (stale/papery/wet cardboard flavors)
