## Session sharegpt_Onuprpa_15: Legal Case Analysis

**Prime Constructions Pty Ltd v Westbridge Investments Pty Ltd [2004] NSWSC:**
- Dispute over settlement agreement for goods sold and delivered
- Original claim: $11,754.60 in Local Court
- Settlement offer: January 21, 2004 (plaintiff's solicitor) - amount claimed, each party pays own costs, deed of release
- Acceptance: January 23, 2004 (defendant's solicitor)
- Subsequent dispute: Defendant proposed additional term regarding letter to proprietor about work quality; reconsidered position
- Deed issues: Document dated February 9, 2004 expressed as deed but improperly executed (only one director signed; Corporations Act 2001 s 127 requires two directors or director + secretary, or sole director if only one)
- Resolution: Deed took effect February 12, 2004 via estoppel or ratification despite defective execution
- Judgment: Declaration that defendant bound by deed; ordered to pay $11,721.60 (amount in deed) plus plaintiff's costs

**Statutory Interpretation (s 127(4) Corporations Act 2001):**
- Cited in Boz One Pty Ltd v McLellan [2015] VSCA 68; 105 ACSR 325 at paragraph 206
- Ambit covers: Common law deed execution method; company constitution prescribed method; estoppel binding situations
- Judicial observations: Young CJ in Eq (Prime Constructions case) noted unclear scope; Einstein J in Guang Dong Zhi Gao Australia Pty Ltd v Fortuna Network Pty Ltd cited with approval; Ann Lyons J in Sunbay Projects Pty Ltd v PR Wieland Holdings Pty Ltd stated no particular formality required for corporate document signing
