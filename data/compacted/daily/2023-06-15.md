## Visual Assistive Technology Research
**Session ID:** sharegpt_bq9G6bT_0  
**Date:** 2023-06-15  
**Topic:** Chapter 2 Background Study - Navigation aids for visually impaired  
**Technical Specifications:**  
- Problem: Real-time object detection for navigation, processing speed critical  
- ICD-11 vision impairment classification: mild (6/12-6/18), moderate (6/18-6/60), severe (6/60-3/60), blindness (below 3/60)  
- Approaches: Visual substitution (focus of study), visual enhancement, visual replacement  
- Subcategories: Electronic Travel Aids (ETA), Electronic Orientation Aids (EOA), Position Locator Devices (PLD)  
**Specific Devices & Studies Cited:**  
- **Head Wear:**  
  - Bai et al. (2019): RGB-D camera, IMU, smartphone, earphone, indoor/outdoor capable; limitations: weak small-obstacle detection, no staircase detection  
  - Jiang et al. (2016): YOLO model, 3D audio via Unity engine, 30fps 1080p, wireless earphones; limitations: 2-5 meter detection range only, ambient sound blocking, information overload with multiple objects  
- **Smart Canes:**  
  - Bharatia et al. (2019): Android app, Google Cloud Vision, ultrasonic sensors, GPS, Bluetooth, voice commands, NLP, face detection, text recognition, traffic light translation; limitations: short sensor coverage, indoor only  
  - WeWalk (YGA non-profit): Touchpad handle, ultrasound sensor, vibration/audio feedback, 500 USD price point, Bluetooth phone pairing, voice assistant; limitations: rain/snow malfunction, loud tip on rough sidewalks  
  - Asati et al. (2019): HR-SO4 ultrasonic sensors, 450-meter range, buzzer alerts, web camera classification; limitations: high cost, no pothole detection, untested in rain  
- **Handheld:**  
  - Dosi et al. (2018): Android app, MobileNets CNN, offline only, requires retraining for new objects  
  - Duman et al. (2019): Raspberry Pi, YOLO, 98.8% distance estimation accuracy, audio feedback; limitations: human detection only, no wearable design  
  - Anish Aralikatti et al. (2020): Android app, OpenCV, Tiny YOLO, FaceNet; limitations: lower accuracy than standard YOLO  
**Design Requirements:** Real-time object detection, multiple feedback mechanisms (audio/vibration toggle), user-friendly interface, contextual information filtering (avoid overwhelming users), privacy/security controls, comfortable public use (non-embarrassing), medical-grade visual replacement alternatives noted
