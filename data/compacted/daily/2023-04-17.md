**Session sharegpt_8fC32jy_7: NSW Subcontractor Statements Legal Content**
Content regarding New South Wales (NSW) Australia subcontractor statements under Workers Compensation Act 1987, Payroll Tax Act 2007, and Industrial Relations Act 1996. Liability rules apply to contracts for work performed after 1 July 2003 where contractor employs workers and work relates to business of retaining business. Exemptions: contractor in receivership/bankruptcy/liquidation, or work unrelated to business (e.g., director's residence). Subcontractor defined broadly as entity engaged under contract; sole traders and partnerships without employees exempt from liability but statement obligation may remain. No equivalent subcontractor statement laws in other Australian states/territories, though joint liability laws exist elsewhere. Statements must relate to period work performed; technically can be provided after work but best practice to obtain before work commences. Retention periods: 5 years (payroll tax), 6 years (wages), 7 years (workers compensation) — maximum 7 years if statement covers all three. Knowingly false statements do not relieve business of liability. Business remains liable if contractor deemed employee regardless of statement. Suggested business actions: review contracts to mandate statements, permit contract termination for non-provision, clarify payment withholding rights for periods lacking statements.

**Session sharegpt_JKWovpl_0: Subcontractor Statements Operational Details**
Subcontractor statements are written contracts between subcontractor and principal contractor declaring compliance with insurance premium payments, payroll tax payments, and remuneration obligations. Principal contractors must obtain statements for specified work periods to avoid liability for subcontractor's workers compensation premiums, payroll tax, and wages. Subcontractors must provide trustworthy statements; false information constitutes offence. Consequences of failure: principal contractor liability for subcontractor's obligations and right to withhold payment until statement provided. Example statement available on NSW government website.
