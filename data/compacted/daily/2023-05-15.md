**Bear Safety Regulations:** Bear canisters mandatory in John Muir Wilderness above 10,000 feet elevation. Minimum capacity 408 cubic inches (6.7 liters). Must meet Interagency Grizzly Bear Committee (IGBC) standards. Store all food, trash, scented items (toothpaste, soap, lotion) in canister or hang 10 feet high, 4 feet from trunk. Bring bear canister as precautionary measure.

**Wilderness Permits:** Required for overnight camping in John Muir Wilderness. Peak season (May 1 to October 31): Wilderness permit required, quota system in place, reserve via Recreation.gov or call (760) 873-2483. Off-peak season (November 1 to April 30): Self-issued permit available at trailhead/visitor center. California Campfire Permit required for campfires regardless of wilderness permit. Separate permit required for Ansel Adams Wilderness.

**John Muir Trail Sections & Waypoints:** Section 1 (Yosemite to Tuolumne Meadows): Donohue Pass (11,066 ft), Lyell Canyon, Tuolumne Meadows (8,600 ft). Section 2 (Tuolumne Meadows to Devils Postpile): Glen Aulin (7,900 ft), Waterwheel Falls, Devils Postpile National Monument (7,560 ft). Section 3 (Devils Postpile to Reds Meadow): Minaret Lake (9,800 ft), Iceberg Lake (10,000 ft), Reds Meadow (7,200 ft). Section 4 (Reds Meadow to Mount Whitney): Crabtree Meadows (10,400 ft), Guitar Lake (11,500 ft), Mount Whitney (14,505 ft).
