User: I'm looking for some advice on how to keep my tomato plants healthy and pest-free. By the way, I just planted 12 new tomato saplings today and I'm excited to see them grow.
Assistant: Congratulations on planting your new tomato saplings! With proper care and attention, they'll thrive and provide you with a bountiful harvest. Here are some tips to keep your tomato plants healthy and pest-free:

**Soil and Watering**

1. **Soil quality**: Tomatoes love well-draining, fertile soil with a pH between 6.0 and 6.8. Add organic matter like compost or manure to improve soil structure and fertility.
2. **Water deeply**: Tomatoes need consistent moisture, especially when fruiting. Water them deeply once or twice a week, depending on weather conditions. Avoid overwatering, which can lead to root rot and other problems.
3. **Mulch around the plants**: Mulch helps retain moisture, suppress weeds, and regulate soil temperature. Use organic mulch like straw, bark chips, or grass clippings around 2-3 inches thick.

**Pest and Disease Prevention**

1. **Keep the area clean**: Remove any debris, weeds, or infested plants to prevent pests and diseases from spreading.
2. **Monitor for pests**: Regularly inspect your plants for signs of pests like aphids, whiteflies, hornworms, and spider mites. Use neem oil or insecticidal soap to control infestations.
3. **Disease prevention**: Inspect your plants for signs of fungal diseases like blight, septoria leaf spot, or powdery mildew. Use copper-based fungicides or bicarbonate-based products to prevent infections.
4. **Crop rotation**: Rotate your tomato plants to a different location each season to avoid depleting the soil of nutrients and to reduce the risk of disease buildup.

**Support and Pruning**

1. **Provide support**: Tomatoes need support as they grow. Use tomato cages, trellises, or stakes to keep them upright and promote even fruiting.
2. **Prune your plants**: Prune your tomato plants to encourage bushy growth, promote air circulation, and increase fruit production. Remove lower leaves, weak growth, and any suckers (shoots that grow between the main stem and a branch).

**Additional Tips**

1. **Sunlight**: Tomatoes need at least 6 hours of direct sunlight per day.
2. **Fertilize**: Feed your tomato plants with a balanced fertilizer (e.g., 10-10-10 NPK) once a month.
3. **Keep the area weed-free**: Weeds compete with your tomato plants for water, nutrients, and light. Regularly remove weeds to promote healthy growth.

By following these tips, you'll be well on your way to growing healthy, pest-free tomato plants. Good luck, and happy gardening!
